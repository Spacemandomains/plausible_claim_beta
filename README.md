The State MachineThe game operates on a simple, sequential state machine that guides the Plaintiff through the necessary legal elements.StatePrompt FocusValidation LogicSTATE 1Protected ActivityChecks for specific actions: Opposition (complaining internally/externally about discrimination) or Participation (filing a charge, testifying, etc.).STATE 2Adverse Employment Action (AEA)Checks for an action that is materially adverse (e.g., termination, demotion, significant change in duties) and is taken by the employer.STATE 3Causal Connection / But-For CauseChecks for temporal proximity (closeness in time between Protected Activity and AEA) and/or circumstantial evidence (e.g., management comments, deviation from policy).FINALRulingLogic executes the Plausibility Tally based on the accumulated facts and provides a final legal assessment.2. Core Logic: Plausibility TallyThe Judge's final ruling is driven by a hidden scoring mechanism‚Äîthe Plausibility Tally. Each specific, non-conclusory fact provided by the Plaintiff accrues points toward a "Plausible" threshold.Fact Specificity: Vague statements ("My boss was mean") score 0 points. Specific details ("On June 1st, I reported harassment to HR") score +1 point.Temporal Proximity: Providing a tight timeline (e.g., AEA occurred $< 2$ months after Protected Activity) scores an additional +2 points in STATE 3.Deviation from Policy: Providing a fact that suggests the employer broke its own rules (e.g., "I was fired without the required written warnings") scores +1 point in STATE 3.$$P_{Total} = \sum_{i=1}^{3} (F_{i} + T_{i} + D_{i})$$Where:$P_{Total}$ is the total Plausibility Score.$F_i$ is the score for specific facts in each state.$T_i$ is the bonus for temporal proximity (only in State 3).$D_i$ is the bonus for policy deviation (only in State 3).3.
